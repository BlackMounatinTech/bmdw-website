A Shovel, a Rake, and a Truck

That's how Black Mountain Dirt Works started. One guy, one idea, and the kind of beginning every good company has.

Four years ago, Michael Mackrell walked into a Home Depot, bought a shovel and a rake, and decided he was going to start a construction company. That was day one of Black Mountain Dirt Works. One truck. One phone. One idea. No investors. No business plan. No backup plan either.

The early jobs were small and hands-on. Clearing lots by hand. Digging trenches that should've been done with an excavator. Doing whatever work he could find while learning the business one project at a time. Michael said yes to every opportunity that came his way, made plenty of mistakes, learned from every single one of them, and put every dollar he earned right back into the company.

Some weeks were good. Some weeks were brutal. Most of the time it was just heads-down work, showing up earlier than the next guy, staying later than the next guy, and figuring out how to do every part of the job better than it had been done before. The kind of grind that doesn't look like much from the outside but builds something real on the inside.

The same guy who started this company is the same guy answering your call today.

Four years later, Black Mountain has grown from one guy with a shovel into a real construction company with experienced crews, the right equipment for the job, and a reputation built on something simple: doing the work properly and finishing what we start. Most of our work comes from referrals and repeat customers — the same people calling us back a year later for the next project, or telling their neighbour about the job we did for them.

That's not an accident. That's what happens when you take the time to do things right and you treat every job like it's the only one that matters. Because the day we stop doing that is the day we stop being worth hiring.

Michael still runs the company day to day. He's on jobsites every week. He's the one answering the phone, walking your property, and making sure the work gets done the way it should. That hands-on approach is what built this company. It's also what keeps it running, and it's what separates Black Mountain from the contractors who started small and forgot where they came from.

How We Work

When you hire Black Mountain, you're dealing directly with the owner. Not a sales team. Not a project coordinator who's never seen a jobsite. You're talking to the guy who built this company from the ground up and still runs every part of it. That direct line is how problems get solved fast and how decisions get made on the spot — not three weeks later after a chain of emails.

Every project starts the same way: a phone call to figure out what you're trying to do, what you've got to work with, and whether we're the right fit for the job. If we are, we'll come out, walk the property, and put together a real plan. If we're not, we'll tell you that too. We don't take on jobs we can't do well.

We run our own crews and we bring in the right equipment for whatever the project needs. Whether it's our machines or contracted equipment for specialized work, every job gets the same treatment: proper prep, proper execution, and proper cleanup when we're done. Our name is on the work, so it has to be right.

What You Can Expect

Honest answers. We'll tell you what something is going to take, what it's going to cost roughly, and what could go wrong before we ever start. No surprises later. If we run into something on site, you'll know about it before we do anything about it.

Quality work. We don't skip the parts of the job that nobody sees. The base prep, the drainage, the compaction — those are the things that determine whether your project lasts five years or fifty. We do them right because they matter.

Communication throughout. You're never wondering what's happening on your property. We send updates, we answer questions, and we're easy to reach when you need us.

A finished job. We don't disappear when the hard part shows up. We don't leave debris behind. We don't say "good enough" and pack up. The job is done when it's actually done — and the customer is happy with how it looks.
