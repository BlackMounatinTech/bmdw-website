Retaining Walls

Block walls, natural boulder walls, and engineered retaining systems built to last.

A retaining wall does more than hold back dirt. It creates usable space, manages water, prevents erosion, and adds permanent value to a property. But most retaining walls fail for the same reasons — bad drainage, no proper base, and walls built without thinking about the loads they actually need to handle. We build walls that don't fail.

What's Included

  • Concrete block and segmental retaining walls
  • Natural boulder walls built from blast rock
  • Engineered retaining systems for steep grades
  • Tear-out and replacement of failed walls
  • Excavation and base preparation
  • Drainage installation behind walls
  • Granular backfill and compaction
  • Wall caps, finishing details, and integration with landscape

How We Do It

1. Site Evaluation & Planning

We look at the slope, the soil, the drainage situation, and what the wall needs to do. Holding three feet of grade is different from holding ten. We figure out the right wall type and the right base depth before we ever put a shovel in the ground.

2. Excavation & Base Prep

We dig a proper trench to undisturbed subgrade, install a compacted gravel base, and lay drainage behind where the wall is going. This is the part most contractors skip. It's also the part that determines whether the wall lasts five years or fifty.

3. Wall Construction & Backfill

We build the wall course by course, setting every block or boulder properly, then backfill with granular material and compact it as we go. The result is a wall that handles the load it was built for and stays standing.

Why It Matters

Failed retaining walls are one of the most expensive problems we get called to fix. By the time a wall is bowing or leaning, the only fix is to tear the whole thing out and start over. The reason walls fail is almost always one of three things: no drainage behind the wall, a bad base, or undersized blocks for the load. We don't skip any of those. Every wall we build is engineered to handle what it's holding back.
