Driveways

Gravel, concrete, and asphalt driveways. New installs, extensions, and replacements.

Your driveway takes more abuse than almost any other part of your property. Every car, every truck, every winter, every spring runoff — it all goes over the same surface. A bad driveway cracks, settles, washes out, and looks like garbage within a few years. A good driveway holds up for decades. The difference is what's underneath.

What's Included

  • New gravel driveways with proper base and compaction
  • Concrete driveways with smooth or exposed aggregate finishes
  • Asphalt driveways and parking pads
  • Driveway extensions and additions
  • Driveway replacement and repair
  • Excavation, sub-base preparation, and grading
  • Geotextile fabric installation for weed and load control
  • Drainage integration to prevent washout

How We Do It

1. Excavate & Prep

We dig down to the right depth — deeper than most people think. We strip the topsoil, shape the sub-grade, and make sure the slope drains water away from your house and your driveway.

2. Base & Fabric

We lay geotextile fabric to keep weeds from coming through and to spread the load, then bring in a proper base of crushed gravel and compact it in lifts. This is the part that determines whether your driveway holds up or sinks.

3. Surface & Finish

We install the finished surface — gravel, concrete, or asphalt — to the right thickness and the right finish. Clean edges, proper drainage, and a surface that's ready to handle whatever you drive on it.

Why It Matters

Most driveway problems happen because the contractor didn't dig deep enough or didn't put down a proper base. We've seen driveways crack within two years because someone poured concrete on six inches of dirt. We do it the right way — proper depth, proper base, proper drainage. The driveway you put in this year should still be holding up when your kids learn to drive.
