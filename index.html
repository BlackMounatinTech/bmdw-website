What We Do

From raw dirt to finished landscape.

Whether it's an overgrown lot, a failing retaining wall, or a backyard you're thinking about redoing — we take on projects from start to finish.

Excavation & Site Prep

Site clearing, earthmoving, cut and fill, trenching, grading, and getting raw land build-ready.

Retaining Walls

Block walls, natural boulder walls, and engineered retaining systems. Built to hold grade and last.

Concrete

Foundations, footings, flatwork, slabs, curbs, and exposed aggregate. From forming to finishing.

Land Clearing & Roads

French drains, perimeter drainage systems, and waterproofing. Keep the water where it belongs.

Water Features

Ponds, waterfalls, and custom water features. Excavation, liner, stonework, and plumbing.

Tree Removal

Safe removal of trees, stumps, and root systems. Clean up and haul away included.

Our Story

Built From Nothing

Four years ago, Michael walked into Home Depot, bought a shovel and a rake, and started Black Mountain Dirt Works. One truck. One phone. A lot of hours and a lot of learning along the way. The business has grown slowly since then, mostly through word of mouth.

The same guy who started this company is the one answering your call today.

Hands-on. Owner-operated from day one. When you call Black Mountain, you're talking to the person who'll be on site. It keeps communication direct and makes it easier to figure things out as the project moves along.

Our Process

How we work.

Every project follows the same three steps. No pressure, just a conversation to see if we're a fit.

1

Call Us

Tell us a bit about your project over the phone. We'll ask some questions and see if it's something we can help with.

2

Site Visit

If it seems like a good fit, we'll come take a look at the property and put together a plan that works for the space and your budget.

3

We Get to Work

Once we've agreed on the plan, we get started. We'll keep you posted as things move along.

Booking Ahead

Our schedule tends to fill up in spring.

If you're thinking about a project for this season, it usually helps to reach out a bit earlier. It gives us time to look at the job and figure out where it fits in.
