Concrete Work

Foundations, footings, flatwork, slabs, curbs, and exposed aggregate. Formed and poured in-house.

Concrete is unforgiving. Once it's poured, you live with it for decades. That's why every concrete pour we do starts with the same thing: taking the time to get the prep work right. Proper sub-base, proper forms, proper reinforcement, proper finish. Skipping any of those steps is how you end up with a slab that cracks, settles, or fails within a few years. We don't skip steps.

What's Included

  • Foundations and footings for new builds and additions
  • Concrete driveways and walkways
  • Patios, slabs, and flatwork of all sizes
  • Concrete curbs and gutter edges
  • Exposed aggregate finishes for a textured, premium look
  • Decorative and stamped concrete options
  • Concrete demolition, removal, and disposal
  • Repair and replacement of existing concrete

How We Do It

1. Site Prep & Excavation

We start by excavating the area to the right depth, removing any organic material, and bringing in proper sub-base material. Compacted base is what keeps concrete from settling or cracking down the line.

2. Forming & Reinforcement

We build the forms to the exact dimensions of your pour, set the rebar or wire mesh where it needs to be, and double-check every measurement. The forms are what make the difference between clean edges and a mess.

3. Pour & Finish

We pour fresh concrete, work it into every corner, and finish the surface to your spec — smooth, broom finish, exposed aggregate, whatever the job calls for. Then we cure it properly so it sets up strong.

Why It Matters

Concrete is one of the most permanent decisions you'll make on your property. A bad pour means cracking, settling, drainage problems, and an eyesore that's expensive to fix. We've seen plenty of jobs done by other contractors that needed to be torn out and redone within five years because the prep work wasn't there. We do it right the first time so you don't have to call anyone again.
